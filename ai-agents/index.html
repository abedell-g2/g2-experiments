AI Agents — Category Page Redesign

Three design concepts for a new AI Agents category page — built around use-case-first navigation, proprietary G2 Agentic Metrics, and buyer-stage awareness. Use case: AI SDR & Outbound Agents.

Why this page needed a complete rethink

What's broken about the current design

  • Star ratings don't capture what matters for autonomous AI — they rate UX, not production reliability
  • "Best AI Agents" ranking is meaningless without use case context — the best SDR agent is not the best DevOps agent
  • No way to surface autonomy level, ramp time, or task completion — the dimensions buyers actually care about
  • Buyers who already have a shortlist are forced through a discovery flow they don't need

G2 Agentic Score — new proprietary metrics

  • Task Completion Rate: % of workflows completed without human rescue (40% weight)
  • Reliability & Error Recovery: Behavior under failure; consistency over time (30%)
  • Business Impact: Time-to-ROI and reviewer-reported outcome quality (30%)
  • Composite = G2 Agentic Score (0–100), collected from structured review questions

Research & design notes

Use case: AI SDR & Outbound Agents

  • A clean, specific, emerging category — distinct from general-purpose AI and established automation tools
  • Products: 11x (Alice), Artisan (Ava), AiSDR, Salesforge (Frank), Reply.io (Jason AI), Regie.ai
  • All product data is illustrative for prototype purposes — not sourced from live G2 reviews
  • G2 Agentic Score methodology is a design proposal, not an active G2 metric

Key buyer insights (from research)

  • 95% of buyers choose from their Day One shortlist — most are validating, not discovering
  • Buyers trust independent review data; vendor-provided metrics rank lowest in credibility
  • Human-in-loop deployments outperform fully autonomous ones 2:1 for ROI — autonomy level is a key purchase criterion
  • Reliability (not intelligence) is the #1 production concern — completion rate > benchmark score
